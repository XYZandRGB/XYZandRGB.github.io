Student Work

Below is a collection of student submissions from a series of courses titled
'Geometry and Mathematics of Design' that I taught and developed curriculum for at Virginia Tech.

Grid Deforming with Parametric Equations

For this exercise, students used Geometry Nodes, a procedural modeling tool in Blender, to alter a grid.
Students then built their own node setups, based on a list of provided equations that mimic famous architectural projects.
This allowed students to experiment with adding new functions, adjusting parameters, and choosing lighting and camera angles.
One of the main goals of this project was to show that
the coordinates of a grid (UV) can be altered to make a 3D shape (XYZ) that cannot be expressed as a single function of U and V.

Tiling According to Conway's Criterion

Conway's Criterion is a list of symmetry requirements for complex shapes to tile the plane.
This is one of many techniques that M.C. Escher used in his tiling work.
Students created their own tiles and patterns.
To serve as inspiration, I made the crocodile tiles (crocotiles) at the top of this page.

Perspective Drawing Using Cross Ratio Calculations

Students learn about more advanced techniques for perspective drawing beyond using parallel lines to draw evenly spaced objects.
Using the cross ratio, students can convert an unlimited number of real-life measurements into their equivalent dimensions in perspective.
Once students calculate the cross ratio by hand, I provide an excel sheet that automatically converts these measurements.
